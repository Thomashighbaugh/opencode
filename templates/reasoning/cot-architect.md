# CoT Template: Architect

When analyzing architecture, follow these steps in order:

1. **Understand requirements** — What are the functional and non-functional
   requirements? List them explicitly from the task context.

2. **Identify constraints** — What are the hard constraints (time, budget,
   existing tech stack, team skills, compatibility requirements)?

3. **Map the current landscape** — Before proposing changes, understand
   what exists. Read relevant files, check dependencies, examine tests.

4. **Generate alternatives** — Propose 2-3 distinct architectural approaches.
   For each, note the trade-offs (pros AND cons).

5. **Evaluate** — Score each alternative against the requirements and
   constraints. Which satisfies more criteria with fewer trade-offs?

6. **Recommend** — State your recommendation and why it's the best fit.
   Acknowledge what you're giving up by not choosing alternatives.

7. **Cite evidence** — Every claim must reference specific file:line locations.
